Diabetes is diagnosed through laboratory tests that measure blood glucose levels and long-term glucose control.

1. Fasting Blood Glucose (FBG): Measures blood glucose after an overnight fast.
   - Normal: 70–99 mg/dL
   - Prediabetes: 100–125 mg/dL
   - Diabetes: ≥126 mg/dL on two separate occasions

2. Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT): Measures blood glucose two hours after drinking a glucose-rich beverage.
   - Diabetes: ≥200 mg/dL at 2 hours
   - Prediabetes: 140–199 mg/dL at 2 hours

3. HbA1c Test: Reflects average blood glucose over 2–3 months.
   - Normal: <5.7%
   - Prediabetes: 5.7–6.4%
   - Diabetes: ≥6.5%

4. Random Blood Glucose Test: Diabetes can be diagnosed if random blood glucose is ≥200 mg/dL in presence of symptoms.

Regular monitoring and early diagnosis are essential to prevent complications. Individuals with prediabetes should undergo lifestyle interventions to prevent progression to diabetes.
