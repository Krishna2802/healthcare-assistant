Symptoms of diabetes vary depending on blood sugar levels and the type of diabetes. Some people with type 2 diabetes may not have any noticeable symptoms for years.

Common symptoms include:

- Frequent urination (polyuria): High blood sugar causes the kidneys to excrete excess glucose, increasing urine production.
- Excessive thirst (polydipsia): Increased urination leads to dehydration, triggering thirst.
- Unexplained weight loss: Particularly common in type 1 diabetes due to the body breaking down fat and muscle for energy.
- Increased hunger (polyphagia): Cells cannot absorb glucose effectively, leading to persistent hunger.
- Fatigue: Lack of glucose in cells causes low energy levels.
- Blurred vision: High blood glucose can cause fluid changes in the lenses of the eyes.
- Slow-healing wounds: Poor circulation and immune system function delay healing.
- Tingling or numbness in hands and feet: Indicates nerve damage (neuropathy).

Other subtle symptoms include frequent infections, such as urinary tract infections, yeast infections, or gum infections. Early detection through regular screening is critical to prevent long-term complications.
