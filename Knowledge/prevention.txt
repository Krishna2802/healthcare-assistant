Type 2 diabetes can often be prevented or delayed through lifestyle changes. Prevention focuses on maintaining healthy weight, physical activity, and a balanced diet.

1. Healthy Diet: Focus on whole foods like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, and healthy fats. Limit refined sugars, processed foods, and saturated fats. Portion control is critical.

2. Regular Physical Activity: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, including aerobic and resistance training, improves insulin sensitivity and aids in weight control.

3. Weight Management: Maintaining a healthy body weight reduces stress on the body's metabolism and improves insulin function.

4. Smoking Cessation: Avoid smoking to improve overall metabolic and cardiovascular health.

5. Blood Pressure and Cholesterol Control: Monitoring and controlling these reduces diabetes risk and prevents cardiovascular complications.

6. Stress Management: Chronic stress can affect blood sugar and metabolic health. Mindfulness, meditation, and adequate sleep are recommended.

7. Routine Checkups: Regular health screenings for blood glucose, HbA1c, blood pressure, and cholesterol help identify prediabetes and early diabetes for timely intervention.

Combining these strategies can prevent or significantly delay the onset of type 2 diabetes.
