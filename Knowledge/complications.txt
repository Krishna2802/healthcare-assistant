Diabetes, if not properly managed, can lead to severe long-term complications affecting multiple organs.

1. Cardiovascular Disease: Increases risk of heart attack, stroke, atherosclerosis, and hypertension. Diabetes accelerates the buildup of plaque in blood vessels.

2. Kidney Damage (Diabetic Nephropathy): High blood sugar damages kidneys over time, potentially leading to kidney failure requiring dialysis.

3. Nerve Damage (Diabetic Neuropathy): High glucose damages nerves, causing pain, tingling, numbness, especially in hands and feet. May lead to foot ulcers or infections.

4. Eye Damage (Diabetic Retinopathy): High glucose levels damage retinal blood vessels, potentially causing blindness. Diabetes also increases cataracts and glaucoma risk.

5. Foot Problems: Poor circulation and neuropathy can lead to infections, ulcers, and sometimes amputations.

6. Skin Conditions: Increased susceptibility to bacterial and fungal infections.

7. Pregnancy Complications: Higher risk of preeclampsia, macrosomia, and gestational diabetes in mother and child.

8. Cognitive Decline: Poorly controlled diabetes may increase risk of dementia and cognitive impairment.

Effective management through diet, exercise, medications, and regular medical checkups can reduce the risk of complications. Patient education is crucial for adherence and self-care.
